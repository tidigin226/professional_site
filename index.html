Nextile

The Nextile is a wearable concept prototype that I created with a small team which won bet hardware hack at CalHacks. It is a shirt with flex sensors and accelerometers. We used machine learning to convert raw sensor data into angles between joints and render a user's real time skeleton. We made the shirt using two edisons which sent sensor data to firebase. After calculating angle data we displayed the user's skeleton using ThreeJs.

 

 
 
 
 
 

SyncSpin

Syncspin is a collaborative music voting web app for listening to music in a group setting. It also uses accelerometer data and visualizations to allow users to sense the excitement of the crowd listening to music. This app won the Mashery API prize at HackGT and was built using MEAN stack.

 

 
 
 
 
 

BeatMyGoal

BeatMyGoal is a web app that allows users to compete with their friends towards a goal for a monetary prize. Users create a goal which friends can join and set a prize using venmo. They log daily progress on the goal. Whoever has the most progress after a certain time or whoever reaches the goal first wins the goal and the prize. This app was created using django and the venmo api.

 

 

Ties

Ties is an android app that motivates you to stay in touch with your contacts. Each contact you select has a time limit to contact by or the contact gets deleted. Everytime you do reach out to a contact, their time limit gets reset. This app one second place at the annual Stokes Hackathon


 
 
 
 
 

StudyBuddy

I created StudyBuddy with three friends at Facebook's Battle of the Bay Hackathon. This was the first big hackathon I went to and and it was an awesome experience. The app helps you find study groups for classes at Berkeley and automatically sets up mailing lists for study groups to use. The app also placed second in the Berkeley Student Technology Council's WebApp Competition.

 

 
 
 
 
 

Asian Americans for Community Involvement

This was an web app I created with a friend for an organization called Asian Americans for Community Involvement . Healthcare is one of the services offered by AACI and we built an app to manage the Patient Navigation Center , one of AACI's healthcare programs. The app allows managers to log appointments of patients who need assistance. Volunteers can then log into the system and sign up to help patints during an appointment. Finally data from appointments are stored for managers to view later.
